**Khanmigo:**

Khanmigo is Khan Academy's AI-powered tutor, built on GPT-4 technology but fine-tuned for education. Unlike regular ChatGPT, it is designed to never give students direct answers, instead using the Socratic method, guiding learners through problems with questions and hints.


Strengths: 
The educational quality is outstanding, with expert educators involved in development, resulting in explanations that aren't just factually accurate but pedagogically sound.
 
Microsoft partnered with Khan Academy in 2024, providing cloud resources via Azure to scale Khanmigo and offering funding to make it free for teachers.


Weaknesses: 
The Socratic method requires patience; for children who get frustrated easily, being asked question after question when they just want help can feel annoying. Some students in testing simply stopped asking Khanmigo for help and went back to watching videos instead.
 Crucially for our problem statement, 
Khan Academy's own CLO reported seeing many chats where students respond "I don't know" or simply disengage, and that some students don't actually have the skills to recognize what they know, what they need to get support on, and how to write good questions to address their weaknesses.
 
Khanmigo only works within Khan Academy — you cannot use it for homework from other sources or general learning outside Khan's content library. If a child's school uses a different curriculum, the alignment may not be perfect.

$4/month for individual learners, $9/month for families, and free for teachers.

### 6. KEY INSIGHT

The research reveals a stunning contradiction at the core of the market: 
although AI-based tutoring improves performance during assisted practice sessions, students relying on the technology may underperform when access is removed — however, carefully designed safeguards, especially asking the AI tutor to provide teacher-designed hints instead of giving away answers, can mitigate these negative effects.
